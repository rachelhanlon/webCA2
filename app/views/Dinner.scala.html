Dinner

Importance of Dinner.......

People who want to lose weight usually skip dinner to cut calories, but they don't realize that this meal is essential too. Learn why dinner is important and how you can make this meal much more healthier.Given the fact that you are not going to eat the next 8 to 10 hours, your body needs the essential nutrients to perform bodily functions and maintain an adequate metabolic rate. Eating a dinner gives you the required carbohydrates needed for this, along with other important vitamins and fiber. Dinner also keeps your blood sugar and insulin levels stable while you are resting.

Thai Green Pork Lettuce Cups

  1. Add oil into a deep saucepan
  2. Cook the pork in the frying pan for 8-10mins until cooked through.
  3. Stir in the green curry paste and 2tbsp of water.
  4. Cook for an aditional 2mins
  5. Remove from the heat and stir in the red onion, lime juice, fish sauce and herbs.
  6. Spoon the pork into the lettuce leaves and serve with rice if you like.

Chicken Satay Salad

  1. Pout the tamari into a large dish and stir in the curry powder, cumin, garlic and honey.
  2. Slice the chicken breasts up and then add to the marinade.
  3. Mix peanutbutter with chili sauce, lime juice and 1tbsp of water to make a spoonable sauce.
  4. Cook the chicken and the marinade together until the chicken in fully cooked.
  5. Add the cooked chicken to the top of some lettuce leaves and spoon over the sauce.

Inside-Out Chicken Kiev

  1. Place the chicken in a baking tray, and rub with a little butter and seasoning to add flavour.
  2. Cook under the grill for 15mins.
  3. Make sure to turn over until cooked through.
  4. Mix together some garlic butter and breadcrumbs.
  5. Remove the chicken from the grill and add the breadcrumb/butter mix.
  6. Return to the grill and cook for a futher 3-5mins until the top is browned. Serve alongside rice or potatoes.

General Information

Exercise

Exercise is also known as physical activity. In simple terms exercise is any movement that works your body at a greater intensity than your usual level of daily activity. Exercise raises your heart rate and works your muscles and is most commonly undertaken to achieve the aim of physical fitness.

Good nutrition is an important part of leading a healthy lifestyle. Combined with physical activity, your diet can help you to reach and maintain a healthy weight, reduce your risk of chronic diseases (like heart disease and cancer), and promote your overall health.

Body Mass Index

Morbid obesity is defined as at least 100 pounds overweight, with a BMI greater than 40. BMI is a measure of your general health and is based on your height and weight ratio. If your BMI is high, you may have an increased risk of developing certain diseases including: high blood pressure.
