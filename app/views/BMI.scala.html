Body Mass Index

BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a guide that shows weight ranges from underweight to obesity. To calculate your BMI you need to know your height and your weight and then divide your weight by your height squared. Or just find your height on our chart below and slide your finger across to find your weight (in pounds). When you have correctly found your height and weight, you will see you will be categorised into different weight ranges. The colour coded weight ranges will give you an indication if you are underweight, normal, overweight or reaching obesity.

Calories

Calories are a measure of how much food and drink contains. The amount of energy you need throughout the day depends on your age, lifestyle and size. The average amount of calories a women should have per day is 2,000Kcal. The average amount of calories a man should have per day is 2,500Kcal. This may vary due to how active the individual is.

Scalabilty

BMI is proportional to mass and inversely proportional to the square of the height. So, if all body dimensions double, and mass scales naturally with the cube of the height, then BMI doubles instead of remaining the same. This results in taller people having a reported BMI that is uncharacteristically high, compared to their actual body fat levels. In comparison, the Ponderal index is based on the natural scaling of mass with the third power of the height. However, many taller people are not just "scaled up" short people but tend to have narrower frames in proportion to their height. Nick Korevaar (a mathematics lecturer from the University of Utah) suggests that instead of squaring the body height (as the BMI does) or cubing the body height (as the Ponderal index does), it would be more appropriate to use an exponent of between 2.3 and 2.7

General Information

Exercise

Exercise is also known as physical activity. In simple terms exercise is any movement that works your body at a greater intensity than your usual level of daily activity. Exercise raises your heart rate and works your muscles and is most commonly undertaken to achieve the aim of physical fitness.
