Exercise

How Often Should you Exercise?

There are 3 specific exercise frequencies that we need to care about most:

  • Overall Exercise Frequency
  • Weight Training Frequency
  • Muscle Group/Body Part Frequency

    • Overall Exercise Frequency

    Overall Exercise frequency can vary as is it depends on the person and the persons goal. For example a fat person with the primary goal of losing fat may have 4 cardio workouts per week, while a skinny person with the primary goal of building muscle may do no cardio whatsoever. But if you are trying to life that healthy lifestyle you should be exercising at very most 6 times a week with one complete rest day.

    Weight Training Frequency

    When it comes to weight training people tend to think that the more they everyday will get the bigger. Which is wrong as the muscles need to rest to gain weight. Thats why the majority of the population should weight train 3-4 times a week and never more than 2 consecutive days in a row.

    Muscle Group/Body Part Frequency

    This is a big one as it needs to be done right. Training one body part more than 2 times a week is bad. Its bad because the muscle is clearly getting no rest so the muscle is not getting the time to form and too much will damage the muscle. Each muscle should be trained once a week for example Arms Monday, legs Tuesday, rest Wedesday, chest Thursday, back Friday, rest Sunday.

    General Information

    Exercise

    Exercise is also known as physical activity. In simple terms exercise is any movement that works your body at a greater intensity than your usual level of daily activity. Exercise raises your heart rate and works your muscles and is most commonly undertaken to achieve the aim of physical fitness.

    Body Mass Index

    Morbid obesity is defined as at least 100 pounds overweight, with a BMI greater than 40. BMI is a measure of your general health and is based on your height and weight ratio. If your BMI is high, you may have an increased risk of developing certain diseases including: high blood pressure.
